General Questions

What is CSI Web App?

CSI Web App is a web based management tool to measure the environmental impacts arising from construction activities. top ^

How much does CSI Web App cost?

CSI Web App is a web based service. Basic subscription cost is per site and depends on the project value category. For large organisations, with multiple sites, we have special discounts available. Contact us for more information. top ^

What do I get from subscribing to CSI Web App?

Subscribers get access to an easy-to-use comprehensive tool set to measure, track, report and manage environmental impacts arising from their site construction activities.

Features include:
- Project use of platform.
- Dynamic reporting and graphing.
- Remote support and help desk.
top ^

Can I try it out before I subscribe?

Yes, you can try out a free demo version. It is fully functional with a test data set. top ^

Is CSI Web App based on accepted GHG accounting standards?

The reporting capability of the CSI Web App follows all relevant UK and international protocols such as the DEFRA emissions factors, ISO 14064-1:2006 and GHG Protocol initiative. top ^

What GHG emission factors does CSI Web App use?

All GHG emission factors used in CSI Web App are based on Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (DEFRA). All emission factors are reviewed each year to update CSI Web App with the most current information available. top ^

What does user permissions mean in CSI Web App?

All users in CSI Web App must be assigned a standard profile which determines specific functions the user can perform, what operations they can perform on data, and which types of data they have access to.

There are four types of standard profiles available: Company Manager, Site Manager, Site Operator and Site Reader.

Company Manager
There is only one "Company Manager" per Company. Company Managers are “Superuser", they can manage users (add/delete users, set their rights) and sites (add/delete sites). They are the default "Site Manager" for all Company sites.

Site Manager
"Site Manager" rights are only granted by explicit permissions given by the "Company Manager". "Site Manager" can be assigned with rights to manage or read a specific Site, it Meters and it Series. Site Manager user cannot delete a Site.

Site Operator
Site Operators are data entry users, they manage series (upload/delete data). Their rights are only granted by explicit permissions given by the "Company Manager".

Site Reader
"Site Reader" rights are only granted by explicit permissions given by the "Company Manager". "Site Reader" can be assigned with rights to read information of a specific Site, including it Meters, Series, Emission Factors and Reports.

Electricity

How is electricity measured?

The main way to measure electricity is the watt-hour. The watt (W) is a measure of electrical power. From light bulbs to different appliances are marked with a watt rating to give an indication of the amount of electrical power they need to work. top ^

What is electricity consumption?

Electricity consumption is a combination of the amount of electricity (that is, the watt) and the period of time for which it’s used. So a standard measure of electricity consumption is the amount of watts used over an hour: the watt-hour (Wh).

An electricity bill records the number of kilowatt-hours (kWh) consumed over a period. top ^

How should electricity data be recorded in the platform?

We recommend to record electricity readings taken from physical meters. A physical meter reading offers you consumption data taken at hourly intervals that could help you to see patterns of your electricity use and find ways to conserve electricity. Your bill, on the other hand, is based on your actual electricity use at the end of each billing period or on your electricity usage patterns in the past. top ^

How CSI Web App calculate electricity source emissions?

CSI Web App uses recorded data on electricity consumption (kWh) and DEFRA´s conversion factor to calculate electricity emissions. top ^

Fuels

Which fuels are included in CSI Web App?

Our CSI tool cover the most common fuels used in the construction industry site activities. The main fuels types are CNG, LPG, Burning Oil, Diesel (average biofuel blend) – Supplied by petrol stations around the UK, Diesel(100% mineral diesel) - Wholesale buy, gas oil and petrol (average biofuel blend and 100% mineral petrol).

The tool can also measure the emission arising from the use of natural gas however, at the moment, the tool is only able to measure it by volume. Either cubic meters or Litres.

Nonetheless we are in the process to updated the tool and create a new meter option to deal exclusively with gas consumption. The tool will convert the volume of gas used to kWh using an appropriated calorific value and convert it to carbon emissions equivalent using the correct emissions factor. top ^

How should fuel data be recorded in the platform?

The best way to record fuel consumption is to use fuel purchase receipts showing the volume of fuel purchased. If you would like to input values extracted from the gas bill, for example, we recommend you to use the actual meter reading in metric units and not the value in kWh. If in doubt please get in touch with us using support@siteimpacts.com and we will be happy to help.

Warning CSI Web App can only record natural gas in metric volume units (litre or cubic meters).

Suppliers measure the gas units you use in either in cubic feet (ft3) or in cubic meters (m3) but this is converted in kWh using he fuel calorific value as one unit of gas does not always produce exactly the same amount of electricity.

You can find gas units in your gas bills to record gas usage but remember you can record metric volume units.

DISCLOSURE NOTE: The CO2 emissions results from the selection of a kWh conversion factor on a gross calorific value (CV) basis can be higher or lower than the CO2 emissions results from CSI Web App depending on the calorific value of the gas used. top ^

Can I use fuel meters for transport?

Yes, if your company has fuel cards you should use a fuel meter to record the emissions (preferred by DEFRA). top ^

How CSI Web App calculate natural gas emissions?

CSI Web App uses recorded data on fuel consumption and DEFRA´s conversion factor to calculate fuel emissions. top ^

How CSI Web App calculate the other fuels emissions?

Transport

How should commercial transport data be recorded in the platform?

You can select and record the type of transport and the kilometers/miles travelled for each delivery. If you mark “round trip” box the platform will calculate a figure of total distance travelled.

OUR ROADMAP: We are working on a functionality to record a multiple delivery route to include the distance travelled to the site (from the previous delivery) plus the distance to the next delivery or return. top ^

How many way are to measure commercial transport?

There are two ways to measure commercial transport available: Distance feature and Location feature.

Distance feature

Distance feature should be used if you know the distance travelled.

Location feature

Location feature works with Google Maps and should be used if you do not know the distance but know the address from the point of origin as long as it is delivered by road.

DISCLOSURE NOTE: All Google Map distances must be taken as estimations so SiteImpacts does not accept any responsibility that may arise for any inaccuracy from it use. top ^

How CSI Web App calculate transport source emissions?

CSI Web App uses recorded data on distance travelled and DEFRA´s conversion factor for most common transport types to calculate transport source emissions.

It must be consider that, to convert distance travelled to CO2e, an uplift of 15% is applied, acording to considered best practice, to account for real world driving conditions.top ^

Waste

How should waste data be recorded in the platform?

You can select and record materials taken off site as a waste using LoW classification measured in tonnes (1 tonne = 1000 kg). top ^

How CSI Web App calculate waste source emissions?

CSI Web App uses recorded data on waste generation and DEFRA´s conversion factor for landfill (‘gate to grave’) to calculate waste source emissions. It must be consider that we have not identified suitable carbon factor options for many waste types.

OUR ROADMAP: We are working on functionalities to record waste generation according to the source activity (Construction Waste, Demolition Waste and Excavation Waste) and waste destination. It will include WRAP standardised volume to mass conversion factors and default waste diversion rates. top ^
